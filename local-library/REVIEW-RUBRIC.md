# Video note review rubric

Use this after a batch ingest to decide what deserves long-term keeping and later Obsidian export.

## Fast decision labels
For each note, choose one:
- **Keep** — strong enough to preserve and likely useful again
- **Maybe** — has some value but needs cleanup or confirmation
- **Skip** — mostly noise, promo, or low transfer value

## Review criteria
Score each area from 0 to 2.

### 1. Signal strength
- **2** — contains concrete workflow, framework, tactic, prompt pattern, or decision rule
- **1** — contains one or two useful ideas but also fluff
- **0** — mostly hype, surface commentary, or recycled obvious advice

### 2. Reusability
- **2** — easy to reuse in your own workflow or turn into an SOP / skill
- **1** — useful, but needs interpretation before reuse
- **0** — too context-specific or too vague to apply again

### 3. Clarity of output
- **2** — note is readable with clean insights and timestamps
- **1** — useful but noisy; needs small cleanup
- **0** — captions/analysis are too messy to trust without heavy editing

### 4. Strategic relevance
- **2** — directly relevant to Hermes, R21 operations, research workflow, or personal knowledge goals
- **1** — indirectly useful or interesting
- **0** — not worth storing long-term for your current direction

## Recommended threshold
- **7–8** → Keep and mark for later Obsidian export
- **5–6** → Maybe; keep local but do not export yet
- **0–4** → Skip or archive only if there is a very specific reason

## What to capture manually when a note is worth keeping
Add a short block under the video in the batch `INDEX.md`:
- **Core takeaway:** one sentence
- **Reusable asset:** prompt, checklist, workflow, or idea worth extracting
- **Next action:** what to test, save, or adapt
- **Why it matters:** why future-you should care

## Strong keep signals
- gives a repeatable workflow
- reveals a useful mental model
- contains a prompt/spec/template worth saving
- changes how you would actually operate
- maps well to Hermes, Obsidian, automation, research, or content systems

## Strong skip signals
- mostly CTA, course funnel, or community promo
- generic inspiration with no usable method
- transcript is too noisy to recover quickly
- duplicates ideas already saved elsewhere
- sounds impressive but does not change any real workflow
